// 01: THE PROBLEM

The AI Energy Arms Race

AI infrastructure and power electronics are scaling faster than wafer supply, energy efficiency, and material availability can support. Silicon is reaching physical limits, while advanced materials like GaN and SiC remain constrained by inefficient, capital-heavy manufacturing. We unlock $30–$70B in potential annual energy savings by upgrading device-level material.

  • Energy bottlenecks
  • Material waste: 500µm vs. 2µm
  • Cost + scaling pressure
// 02: THE PROCESS

Proton Beam Precision

Rayton replaces wasteful diamond-wire cutting with a proprietary high-current proton beam process. Hydrogen ions are implanted into GaN or SiC crystals, enabling controlled separation of ultra-thin 2µm active layers without mechanical grinding.

  • 2µm active layers
  • No mechanical sawing
  • Lower material loss
// 03: THE MULTIPLIER

Turn One Wafer Into Ten

One conventional wafer becomes 10 or more ultra-thin wafers with the same electrical performance. This dramatically increases material efficiency while reducing exposure to supply shortages, tariffs, and years-long fab expansion cycles.

  • 10× or more wafer output
  • 30% lower input costs
  • 100% drop-in compatibility
